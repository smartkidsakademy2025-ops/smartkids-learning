Digital Safety • Essential Guide

The Parent's Guide to
Internet Safety.

As our children spend more time learning and playing online, the "digital playground" requires new rules to keep them protected. Here is how we teach safety at Kidventor Academy.

1. The Privacy Shield

At Kidventor, we teach students the "Stranger Danger" of the 21st century. Personal information—like your full name, home address, school location, or phone number—should never be shared online. We encourage parents to check privacy settings on all apps and games regularly.

2. Identifying "Digital Traps"

Children are often targets for phishing and malicious pop-ups disguised as free games or prizes. We train our students to recognize these "traps." The rule is simple: If you didn't look for it, don't click it.

The Kidventor Pledge

"I will never share my password with anyone except my parents. I will always ask for permission before downloading a new app."

3. Digital Empathy & Bullying

Safety isn't just about protecting yourself; it's about how you treat others. Our moral lessons curriculum emphasizes that cyberbullying is never acceptable. We teach children to be "Upstanders"—leaders who speak up when they see someone being treated unfairly online.

4. Safe Social Media Habits

While many platforms have age limits, children often find their way onto social media early. We suggest keeping computers in common areas (like the living room) rather than bedrooms to ensure an open, supervised environment.
