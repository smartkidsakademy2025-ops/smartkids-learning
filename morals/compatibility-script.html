Love Beyond Borders

A Modern African Family Drama

Act I — The Foundation

Two lovers marry despite tribal tension. Adiza is from the North. Obalade is Yoruba from the Southwest. Their fathers support the union. Their mothers worry in different ways.

They had no mansion, no luxury car, no riches. But they had understanding. They chose patience over pressure.

ADIZA

We will build gradually. No rushing. Life is not a race.

OBALADE

As long as we have peace, we are already rich.

Act II — The Steering Wheel

Adiza becomes Store Manager. The company driver resigns suddenly. Deliveries must continue.

TINUKE

Madam, you want to drive by yourself?

ADIZA

Customers will not listen to excuses. Load the bus.

On the road, she finds Obalade waiting for transport.

OBALADE

You look stunning in that cap. Let me drive. Today, I am your staff.

ADIZA

At work, I am your boss. At home, we settle later.

Act III — The Mothers

Adiza's mother arrives unexpectedly and questions everything: jobs, grandchildren, timing, tradition.

MUM ADIZA

Where are my grandchildren? Why are you delaying life?

ADIZA

Mum, we are planning wisely, not delaying life.

MAAMI

Marriage is not competition. But do not let small issues become big ones.

Act IV — Office Fire

At the monthly review, HR publicly queries Obalade for late delivery.

HR (ABIGAIL)

Why was Wednesday delivery delayed? Why should your salary not be cut?

OBALADE

The tyre deflated after loading. I off-loaded, repaired it, then loaded again.

HR

Take this query letter.

He suspects she wants to expose that he and Adiza are husband and wife.

Act V — The Taxi

After work, both return home silently in a taxi.

Without speaking, Obalade reaches for Adiza's hand. She pulls him close and rests on his shoulder.

TAXI DRIVER

Oga, wait till you reach house now!

They laugh—then Adiza suddenly holds her stomach in pain.

Act VI — New Life

At the clinic, the doctor calls Obalade inside.

DOCTOR

Congratulations. She is six weeks pregnant. She needs rest.

OBALADE

Pregnant?

ADIZA

Looks like our mothers will win this round.

Outside, the taxi driver is still waiting.

TAXI DRIVER

We must look after each other. In this country, people must be cameras for one another.

They drive home smiling into a new chapter of life.
