The Compatibility Script

A Drama of Tribes, Jobs, and the Power of Partnership

— ACT 01: THE FOUNDATION —

The story begins with two young couples. Religion and tribe were the first barriers, but they were settled by fathers who were not biased. Even the groom’s mother supported the marriage, though the bride’s mother remained skeptical.

The siblings often made matters worse due to separate experiences in their universities. But Obalade and Adiza didn't look at the family ends of the issues. Each time they were together, they laughed the matters away.

One year into the marriage. No "good jobs" yet, and no rush for babies. They understood that life is gradual. Then, Adiza secures a job as a Store Manager.
ADIZA

(To herself) I cannot inform my mother that I have a job before my husband. It will cause a "system error" in her head. For now, I will just support my mother-in-law at the market.

3 months later. The company driver, Kazim, resigns suddenly for a bank job.
ADIZA

But Kazim, this is too close! You are leaving now? I am left with six female staff with no driving experience. We have supplies to make!

TINUKE (Staff)

Madam, you want to supply yourself? You look stunning in that driver uniform o! Sexy!

ADIZA

What will I do? If this were my personal business, I wouldn't sit watching it go down. Please load the mini-bus. I am moving!

Adiza is driving the truck when she sees Obalade by the road.
OBALADE

(Surprised) Adi?! My love, why? (He bursts into laughter seeing the company cap) Wao! You look stunning!

ADIZA

(In Pidgin) Na so e be oo! Kazim resigned this morning. Please enter inside. Where are you going?

OBALADE

To the market for accessories. But move over—let me drive you. I can help with this today. My repairs can wait until the weekend. Today, the Manager needs a Driver.

— ACT 02: THE MOTHER'S VISIT —

ADIZA

(Watching Obalade) You look just like my grandpa did for my grandma. He was such an amazing man. My dad supported my mum always, and that support earned me my certificates.

OBALADE

My love, I can do this work. The owners are abroad; you have the right to employ who you like. I can earn more, and we can save for our own store. I’ll be your boss at home, but you are my boss at work.

The staff learns from their cooperation. But then... Adiza’s mother visits suddenly.
MUM ADIZA

Don't tell me you work in the same place! Adiza, why didn't you inform me you were coming? Obalade, why are you prostrating in a driver uniform?

Later that night at dinner...
MUM ADIZA

Where are my grandchildren? (To Obalade) Sir! Sit there! So you cannot get a woman pregnant? (She begins rebuking them in Hausa).

The next morning. A phone call from Adiza's Father reveals the truth.
ADIZA

(To her Mother) Dad says you have been in Lagos for four days? But you just came to my house yesterday! You told him I was pregnant just so you could go to parties with your friends?

MUM ADIZA

I had to see how you were doing! And I still want to visit your workplace.

ADIZA

Mum, Dad misses you in Abuja. He missing his wife. Please, go back to him. My mother-in-law is caring, and we are fine. Your "grandchild" story will have to wait for the right time!

Adiza gestures for Obalade to head to the truck. The "Mother Error" is resolved—for now.

KidVentor Logic Board

Zero-Bias Partnership

Obalade and Adiza show that a "Team" doesn't care about tribes or who is the "boss." They care about the Success of the System.

Handling Noise

Family pressure is often like "background noise" in a signal. You must filter it out to keep the connection strong.
