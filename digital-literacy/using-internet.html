Using the Internet 🌐

Learn how the internet helps students study, communicate, research, and grow in the digital world safely and responsibly.

Introduction

The internet is one of the most powerful tools in the modern world. It connects computers, phones, tablets, and people across the globe. With the internet, students can learn new things, teachers can teach online, businesses can grow, and families can communicate from different countries.

Learning how to use the internet correctly is an important part of digital literacy.

What is the Internet?

The internet is a worldwide network of connected computers and devices that share information.

  • Search for information
  • Watch educational videos
  • Attend online classes
  • Send emails and messages
  • Download files
  • Read news
  • Buy and sell online
  • Connect with people around the world

Common Ways We Use the Internet

1. Searching for Information

Students use search engines like Google to find answers for assignments, research, and projects.

2. Online Learning

The internet helps students learn from anywhere through videos, Zoom classes, educational websites, and apps.

3. Communication

People use emails, WhatsApp, video calls, and school portals to stay connected quickly and easily.

4. Entertainment

The internet also provides movies, music, games, and stories—but it should be balanced with learning.

Important Internet Terms

Website

A place on the internet where information is stored.

Browser

An app used to open websites like Chrome, Edge, Firefox, and Safari.

Search Engine

A tool that helps you find information online such as Google, Bing, and Yahoo.

Link

A clickable connection that takes you from one webpage to another.

Good Habits When Using the Internet

  • Search carefully
  • Read trusted websites
  • Use educational content often
  • Respect others online
  • Avoid dangerous websites
  • Never share private information carelessly

Summary

The internet is a powerful tool for learning, communication, and growth. When used wisely, it helps students become smarter, faster, and better prepared for the future.

Use the internet to build your future, not to waste your future.
