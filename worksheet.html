Computer Science 101

The Sandwich Method

Master the art of Computational Thinking by breaking big problems into delicious, bite-sized steps.

An algorithm is simply a **step-by-step recipe** for a computer. Computers aren't smart like humans; they can't "guess" what you want. You must tell them exactly what to do, in the exact right order.
Think of it this way: A computer is a robot that follows a treasure map. If the map says "Turn Left" instead of "Turn Right," the robot will never find the gold!

Making a sandwich seems easy, but to a computer, it's 50 different movements. **Decomposition** is the act of breaking a big job (Making Lunch) into tiny jobs (Opening the Bread Bag).

  • 1 Get the bread and plate.
  • 2 Spread the peanut butter.
  • 3 Assemble and cut.

When an algorithm fails, we call it a **Bug**. Imagine your "Getting Ready for School" algorithm:

The Buggy Way

1. Put on shoes.
2. Put on socks.

The Fixed Way

1. Put on socks.
2. Put on shoes.
